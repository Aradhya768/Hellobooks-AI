# Balance Sheet

## What is a Balance Sheet?
A Balance Sheet is a financial statement that provides a snapshot of a company's financial position at a specific point in time. It lists what the company owns (assets), what it owes (liabilities), and the owners' stake (equity).

## The Fundamental Equation
**Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders' Equity**

This equation must always be in balance — hence the name "Balance Sheet."

## Structure

### ASSETS (What the company OWNS)

#### Current Assets (converted to cash within 1 year)
- Cash and Cash Equivalents
- Accounts Receivable (money owed by customers)
- Inventory
- Prepaid Expenses
- Short-term Investments

#### Non-Current Assets (long-term)
- Property, Plant & Equipment (PP&E)
- Less: Accumulated Depreciation
- Intangible Assets (patents, trademarks, goodwill)
- Long-term Investments

### LIABILITIES (What the company OWES)

#### Current Liabilities (due within 1 year)
- Accounts Payable (money owed to suppliers)
- Short-term Loans
- Accrued Expenses
- Deferred Revenue
- Current portion of long-term debt

#### Non-Current Liabilities (due after 1 year)
- Long-term Debt / Bonds Payable
- Deferred Tax Liabilities
- Pension Obligations

### SHAREHOLDERS' EQUITY (Owners' Stake)
- Common Stock / Paid-in Capital
- Retained Earnings (accumulated profits not distributed)
- Less: Treasury Stock (shares bought back)

## Key Balance Sheet Ratios
| Ratio | Formula | Interpretation |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | Liquidity (>1 is healthy) |
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Debt / Total Equity | Financial leverage |
| Working Capital | Current Assets - Current Liabilities | Short-term financial health |
| Return on Assets | Net Income / Total Assets | Asset efficiency |

## Example Balance Sheet
```
ASSETS
Current Assets:
  Cash                    $20,000
  Accounts Receivable     $15,000
  Inventory               $25,000
Total Current Assets:     $60,000

Non-Current Assets:
  Equipment               $50,000
  Less: Depreciation     ($10,000)
Total Assets:            $100,000

LIABILITIES
Current Liabilities:
  Accounts Payable        $12,000
  Short-term Loan         $8,000
Total Current Liab.:      $20,000

Long-term Debt:           $30,000
Total Liabilities:        $50,000

EQUITY
  Owner's Capital         $35,000
  Retained Earnings       $15,000
Total Equity:             $50,000

Total Liabilities + Equity: $100,000 ✓
```
