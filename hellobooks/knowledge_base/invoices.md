# Invoices

## What is an Invoice?
An invoice is a commercial document issued by a seller to a buyer, detailing the products or services provided and the amount owed. It serves as a formal payment request and a record of the sale.

## Key Components of an Invoice
1. **Invoice Number**: Unique identifier for tracking
2. **Invoice Date**: When the invoice was issued
3. **Due Date**: Payment deadline (e.g., Net 30 = 30 days from invoice date)
4. **Seller Information**: Name, address, contact, tax ID
5. **Buyer Information**: Name, address, billing details
6. **Line Items**: Description, quantity, unit price, subtotal
7. **Subtotal**: Total before taxes/discounts
8. **Tax**: GST, VAT, or applicable sales tax
9. **Total Amount Due**: Final amount payable
10. **Payment Terms & Methods**: Bank transfer, credit card, cheque, etc.

## Types of Invoices
- **Standard Invoice**: Most common; used for one-time transactions
- **Proforma Invoice**: Preliminary bill sent before goods/services are delivered
- **Recurring Invoice**: Sent on a regular basis for ongoing services
- **Credit Note (Credit Invoice)**: Issued to reduce a previously billed amount
- **Debit Note**: Issued to increase a previously billed amount

## Common Payment Terms
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| Net 7 | Payment due within 7 days |
| Net 30 | Payment due within 30 days |
| Net 60 | Payment due within 60 days |
| 2/10 Net 30 | 2% discount if paid within 10 days, else full payment in 30 |
| Due on Receipt | Payment immediately upon receiving invoice |

## Invoice Lifecycle
1. Service/product delivered
2. Invoice created and sent to client
3. Client reviews and approves
4. Payment processed
5. Receipt/acknowledgement issued
6. Recorded in accounts receivable

## Accounts Receivable vs Accounts Payable
- **Accounts Receivable (AR)**: Money owed TO your business (you issued the invoice)
- **Accounts Payable (AP)**: Money your business OWES (you received the invoice)
