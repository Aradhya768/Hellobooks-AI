# Cash Flow Statement

## What is a Cash Flow Statement?
A Cash Flow Statement tracks the actual movement of cash into and out of a business over a specific period. Unlike P&L which includes non-cash items, the cash flow statement only records real cash movements.

**Key Principle**: A profitable business can still go bankrupt if it runs out of cash (e.g., slow-paying customers, over-investment in inventory).

## The Three Sections

### 1. Operating Activities (Core Business)
Cash flows from day-to-day business operations.

**Cash Inflows:**
- Cash received from customers
- Interest received

**Cash Outflows:**
- Cash paid to suppliers
- Cash paid to employees (salaries)
- Tax payments
- Interest paid

**Net Cash from Operations** = Cash In - Cash Out

### 2. Investing Activities (Assets)
Cash flows related to buying/selling long-term assets.

**Cash Inflows:**
- Sale of equipment or property
- Sale of investments
- Loan repayments received

**Cash Outflows:**
- Purchase of equipment/property (CapEx)
- Purchase of investments
- Loans given to others

**Net Cash from Investing** (usually negative for growing companies)

### 3. Financing Activities (Capital Structure)
Cash flows related to funding the business.

**Cash Inflows:**
- Bank loans received
- Issuing shares (equity financing)

**Cash Outflows:**
- Repaying loans
- Dividend payments to shareholders
- Share buybacks

**Net Cash from Financing**

## Final Cash Position
```
Opening Cash Balance          $10,000
+ Net Cash from Operations    $25,000
+ Net Cash from Investing    ($15,000)
+ Net Cash from Financing    ($5,000)
= Closing Cash Balance        $15,000
```

## Free Cash Flow (FCF)
**FCF = Net Cash from Operations - Capital Expenditures**

FCF represents cash available after maintaining/expanding the asset base. It's a key indicator of business health and the ability to:
- Pay dividends
- Repay debt
- Invest in growth
- Weather downturns

## Cash Flow vs Profit
| Aspect | Cash Flow | Profit (P&L) |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Basis | Actual cash received/paid | Accrual (when earned/incurred) |
| Depreciation | Not included | Included as expense |
| Inventory purchase | Reduces cash immediately | Only reduces profit when sold |
| Loan repayment | Reduces cash | Does NOT reduce profit |
| Timing | When cash moves | When transaction occurs |

## Key Cash Flow Ratios
- **Operating Cash Flow Ratio** = Operating CF / Current Liabilities (liquidity)
- **Cash Flow Margin** = Operating CF / Revenue (cash generation efficiency)
- **Cash Return on Assets** = Operating CF / Total Assets
