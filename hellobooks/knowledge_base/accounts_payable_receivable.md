# Accounts Payable & Accounts Receivable

## Accounts Receivable (AR)

### Definition
Money that customers OWE your business for goods/services already delivered but not yet paid for. AR is an **asset** on the balance sheet.

### AR Process
1. Sale made on credit → Invoice issued
2. Invoice recorded in AR ledger
3. Payment received → AR reduced, Cash increased
4. If unpaid past due date → Follow up / collections

### Key AR Metrics
- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)** = (AR / Total Credit Sales) × Number of Days
  - Lower DSO = Faster collections = Better cash flow
  - Industry benchmark: typically 30–45 days

- **Aging Report**: Categorizes outstanding invoices by age
  | Age | Risk Level |
  |-----|-----------|
  | 0–30 days | Low |
  | 31–60 days | Medium |
  | 61–90 days | High |
  | 90+ days | Very High / Consider write-off |

### Bad Debt
When receivables cannot be collected:
- **Allowance Method**: Estimate uncollectible amount in advance (matching principle)
- **Direct Write-off**: Remove from books when confirmed uncollectible
- Bad debt is recorded as an expense on the P&L

## Accounts Payable (AP)

### Definition
Money YOUR business OWES to suppliers/vendors for goods/services received but not yet paid. AP is a **liability** on the balance sheet.

### AP Process
1. Purchase made on credit → Supplier invoice received
2. Invoice verified (3-way match: PO, receipt, invoice)
3. Recorded in AP ledger
4. Payment made on due date → AP reduced, Cash reduced

### Key AP Metrics
- **Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)** = (AP / COGS) × Number of Days
  - Higher DPO = Holding cash longer = Good for cash flow
  - But too high can damage supplier relationships

### AP Best Practices
- Capture early payment discounts (e.g., 2/10 Net 30)
- Avoid late payments (protects credit terms)
- Automate invoice approval workflows
- Reconcile AP ledger with supplier statements monthly

## Working Capital Management

**Working Capital = Current Assets - Current Liabilities**
**= (Cash + AR + Inventory) - (AP + Short-term Debt)**

### The Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)
**CCC = DIO + DSO - DPO**
- **DIO** (Days Inventory Outstanding): How long inventory is held
- **DSO**: How fast customers pay
- **DPO**: How long before you pay suppliers

**Lower CCC = More efficient cash management**

### Example
- DIO = 30 days (inventory sold in 30 days)
- DSO = 45 days (customers pay in 45 days)
- DPO = 40 days (pay suppliers in 40 days)
- **CCC = 30 + 45 - 40 = 35 days**

The business needs 35 days of financing for each cash cycle.
