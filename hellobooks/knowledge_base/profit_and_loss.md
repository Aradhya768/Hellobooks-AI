# Profit & Loss Statement (Income Statement)

## What is a P&L Statement?
A Profit & Loss (P&L) statement, also called an Income Statement, summarizes a company's revenues, costs, and expenses over a specific period (monthly, quarterly, or annually). It shows whether the business made a profit or a loss.

## Structure of a P&L Statement

### 1. Revenue (Top Line)
Total income generated from business operations.
- Gross Sales
- Less: Returns & Discounts
- **Net Revenue**

### 2. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)
Direct costs to produce goods or services sold.
- Raw Materials
- Direct Labor
- Manufacturing Overhead

### 3. Gross Profit
**Gross Profit = Net Revenue - COGS**

### 4. Operating Expenses (OPEX)
Indirect costs of running the business:
- Salaries & Wages
- Rent & Utilities
- Marketing & Advertising
- Depreciation & Amortization
- Administrative Expenses

### 5. Operating Income (EBIT)
**Operating Income = Gross Profit - Operating Expenses**

### 6. Non-Operating Items
- Interest Income/Expense
- Gains/Losses on asset sales

### 7. Net Profit (Bottom Line)
**Net Profit = Operating Income ± Non-Operating Items - Taxes**

## Key Profitability Ratios
| Ratio | Formula | What it Shows |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Gross Margin | Gross Profit / Revenue × 100 | Production efficiency |
| Operating Margin | Operating Income / Revenue × 100 | Operational efficiency |
| Net Profit Margin | Net Profit / Revenue × 100 | Overall profitability |

## Example P&L (Simple)
```
Revenue:              $100,000
COGS:                 ($40,000)
Gross Profit:          $60,000  (60% margin)

Operating Expenses:   ($35,000)
Operating Income:      $25,000  (25% margin)

Interest Expense:      ($2,000)
Tax (20%):             ($4,600)
Net Profit:            $18,400  (18.4% margin)
```

## P&L vs Balance Sheet
- **P&L**: Shows performance over a TIME PERIOD (how much you earned/spent)
- **Balance Sheet**: Shows financial position at a POINT IN TIME (what you own/owe)
