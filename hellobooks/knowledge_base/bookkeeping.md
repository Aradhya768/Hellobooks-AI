# Bookkeeping Basics

## What is Bookkeeping?
Bookkeeping is the process of recording all financial transactions of a business systematically. It forms the foundation of accounting and ensures accurate financial records.

## Key Concepts

### Double-Entry Bookkeeping
Every transaction affects at least two accounts. For every debit entry, there must be a corresponding credit entry of equal value.
- **Debit**: Left side of a ledger entry; increases assets and expenses
- **Credit**: Right side of a ledger entry; increases liabilities, equity, and revenue

### Chart of Accounts
A complete list of all accounts used by a business:
- **Assets**: Cash, Accounts Receivable, Inventory, Equipment
- **Liabilities**: Accounts Payable, Loans, Accrued Expenses
- **Equity**: Owner's Capital, Retained Earnings
- **Revenue**: Sales, Service Income
- **Expenses**: Rent, Salaries, Utilities, Cost of Goods Sold

### The Accounting Equation
**Assets = Liabilities + Owner's Equity**

This equation must always remain balanced after every transaction.

## Common Bookkeeping Tasks
1. Recording daily transactions in a journal
2. Posting journal entries to the general ledger
3. Reconciling bank statements monthly
4. Generating trial balance to check accuracy
5. Preparing financial statements

## Best Practices
- Record transactions promptly and accurately
- Keep business and personal finances separate
- Reconcile accounts regularly (monthly at minimum)
- Maintain organized receipts and supporting documents
- Back up financial data regularly
