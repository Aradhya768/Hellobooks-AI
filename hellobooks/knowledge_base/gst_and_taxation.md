# GST & Business Taxation

## What is GST?
Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services. It is a destination-based, multi-stage tax collected at each stage of the supply chain.

## GST Structure (India)
- **CGST**: Central GST (collected by Central Government)
- **SGST**: State GST (collected by State Government)
- **IGST**: Integrated GST (for inter-state transactions; collected by Centre)

### GST Rate Slabs
| Rate | Category |
|------|---------|
| 0% | Essential goods (food grains, milk, eggs) |
| 5% | Basic necessities, economy travel |
| 12% | Processed foods, business class travel |
| 18% | Most services, electronics, software |
| 28% | Luxury goods, automobiles, tobacco |

## Input Tax Credit (ITC)
Businesses can offset GST paid on purchases (inputs) against GST collected on sales (outputs).

**GST Payable = Output GST - Input Tax Credit**

**Example:**
- Purchased raw materials: ₹1,00,000 + ₹18,000 GST
- Sold finished goods: ₹1,50,000 + ₹27,000 GST
- ITC Available: ₹18,000
- **Net GST Payable = ₹27,000 - ₹18,000 = ₹9,000**

## GST Compliance
- **GSTIN**: 15-digit GST Identification Number required for registration
- **GST Returns**: Regular filings (GSTR-1, GSTR-3B)
- **Threshold**: Businesses with turnover > ₹40 lakh (goods) / ₹20 lakh (services) must register
- **Composition Scheme**: Small businesses (<₹1.5 crore turnover) can pay flat rate

## Income Tax for Businesses

### Business Structures & Tax Rates (India)
| Structure | Tax Rate |
|-----------|---------|
| Sole Proprietorship | Slab rates (same as individual) |
| Partnership Firm | 30% flat |
| Private Limited Company | 22% (existing) / 15% (new manufacturing) |
| LLP | 30% flat |

### Deductible Business Expenses
- Rent for business premises
- Salaries and wages
- Depreciation on assets
- Interest on business loans
- Marketing and advertising
- Professional fees
- Travel and conveyance

### Tax Deadlines (India)
- **Advance Tax**: Quarterly payments (15 Jun, 15 Sep, 15 Dec, 15 Mar)
- **ITR Filing**: 31 July (non-audit), 31 October (audit cases)
- **TDS Return**: Quarterly

## TDS (Tax Deducted at Source)
When making certain payments (salary, rent, professional fees), businesses must deduct tax at source and deposit with government.
- TDS on Salary: As per income tax slab
- TDS on Professional Fees: 10%
- TDS on Rent (above ₹2.4 lakh/year): 10%
