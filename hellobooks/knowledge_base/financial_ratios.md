# Financial Ratios & Analysis

## Why Financial Ratios Matter
Financial ratios help stakeholders (investors, managers, lenders) quickly assess a company's health by comparing numbers from financial statements. They enable benchmarking against industry peers and tracking performance over time.

## Categories of Ratios

### 1. Liquidity Ratios (Short-term solvency)
Measure ability to meet short-term obligations.

| Ratio | Formula | Healthy Range |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5 – 3.0 |
| Quick Ratio (Acid Test) | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | > 1.0 |
| Cash Ratio | Cash / Current Liabilities | > 0.5 |

### 2. Profitability Ratios
Measure ability to generate profit.

| Ratio | Formula | What it Means |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Gross Profit Margin | Gross Profit / Revenue × 100 | % revenue after production costs |
| Net Profit Margin | Net Profit / Revenue × 100 | % revenue kept as profit |
| Return on Assets (ROA) | Net Income / Total Assets × 100 | Asset utilization efficiency |
| Return on Equity (ROE) | Net Income / Shareholders' Equity × 100 | Return to shareholders |
| EBITDA Margin | EBITDA / Revenue × 100 | Operating profitability |

### 3. Leverage / Solvency Ratios (Long-term solvency)
Measure debt burden and financial structure.

| Ratio | Formula | Healthy Range |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Debt / Total Equity | < 2.0 (varies by industry) |
| Debt Ratio | Total Debt / Total Assets | < 0.5 |
| Interest Coverage | EBIT / Interest Expense | > 3.0 |
| Equity Multiplier | Total Assets / Total Equity | Lower = less leveraged |

### 4. Efficiency / Activity Ratios
Measure how effectively assets are used.

| Ratio | Formula | Better Direction |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Inventory Turnover | COGS / Average Inventory | Higher = faster selling |
| Asset Turnover | Revenue / Total Assets | Higher = more efficient |
| Receivables Turnover | Net Credit Sales / Average AR | Higher = faster collection |
| Payables Turnover | COGS / Average AP | Lower = slower payment (better for cash) |

### 5. Valuation Ratios (for public companies)
| Ratio | Formula | Use |
|-------|---------|-----|
| P/E Ratio | Market Price / EPS | Growth expectations |
| Price-to-Book | Market Cap / Book Value | Valuation vs assets |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise Value / EBITDA | Acquisition valuation |

## DuPont Analysis
Breaks ROE into three components:
**ROE = Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier**

This helps identify whether ROE comes from profitability, efficiency, or leverage.

## Red Flags in Financial Ratios
- Current ratio < 1 (can't cover short-term debts)
- Declining gross margins over time
- DSO increasing (customers paying slower)
- Interest coverage < 1.5 (struggling to pay interest)
- Negative free cash flow with positive net income (earnings quality issue)
